What is Cardiac Surgery?

Cardiovascular surgery, also known as cardiac or heart surgery, encompasses any surgical procedure involving the heart or the blood vessels responsible for transporting blood to and from the heart.

John Hopkins Medicine

Surgical Procedures

Coronary Artery Bypass Graft (CABG)

In a coronary artery bypass graft procedure, a blood vessel is taken from another part of the body (typically the chest, leg, or arm) and connected to the coronary artery both above and below the narrowed or blocked region. This newly attached blood vessel is referred to as a graft. The quantity of grafts required is determined by the severity of your coronary heart disease and the number of narrowed coronary blood vessels. The procedure is conducted under general anesthesia.

National Health Service (NHS)
Heart Valve Replacement

The heart is made up of four valves. A valve consists of small flaps of tissue that open and close to facilitate the one-way flow of blood.

Heart valve surgery is done when one or more of the valves is diseased or damaged as it can affect blodflow. It can affect blood flow in two ways:

  • When a valve fails to fully open or becomes rigid, it can obstruct the normal flow of blood, a condition known as valve stenosis or narrowing.

  • When a valve fails to close properly, it permits the backward flow of blood, a condition referred to as valve regurgitation, or a leaky valve.

British Heart Foundation (BHF)
Septal Defect Repair

A septal defect occurs when there is an abnormality in the wall (septum) between the chambers of the heart. There are two main types of septal defect:

  • An atrial septal defect (ASD) is characterized by a hole between the left and right atria, allowing extra blood to flow into the right side of the heart, leading to its stretching and enlargement.

  • A ventricular septal defect (VSD) is a common congenital heart condition where a hole exists between the left and right ventricles, causing extra blood to flow from the left to the right ventricle due to pressure differences, leading to increased lung pressure and stretching of the left-sided pumping chamber.

National Health Service (NHS)
